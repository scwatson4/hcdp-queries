March 13, 2026 was almost certainly the last time Manoa Valley experienced flooding conditions. Lyon Arboretum recorded 198mm (7.8 in) that day on top of already-saturated soil (64% VWC from two prior heavy rain days), with 376mm total over Mar 11-14. All Oahu stations exceeded 100mm simultaneously, with heavy rain across the entire upstream watershed (Kalawahine 218mm, Nuuanu Reservoir 177mm, Upper Waolani 174mm). The comparable January 26, 2025 event (195mm) fell on much drier soil (46% VWC) with no preceding multi-day rain, so the ground absorbed most of it.
