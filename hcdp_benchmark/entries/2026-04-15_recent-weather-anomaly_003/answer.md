A major multi-island deluge on March 13-14, 2026. Nahuku (Big Island) recorded 711 mm (28 inches) in a single day — the highest single-day reading in the database. 46 of 74 stations (62%) recorded over 100 mm, and 15 stations exceeded 250 mm (10 inches). The event hit Big Island, Maui, and Oahu simultaneously, with a two-day network total of 21,443 mm — roughly 8x a normal March day. This was likely a Kona low or similar synoptic-scale storm system.
