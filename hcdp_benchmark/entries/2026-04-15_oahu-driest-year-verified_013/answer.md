2022 was Oahu's driest calendar year in this database — 985 mm station-average, 67.5% of the long-term mean and 10 percentage points below the next-driest year (2000 at 77.3%). The result is robust to a network-composition stress test: a stable panel of 41 stations with continuous 20+ year records and reporting in 2022 confirms the ranking. USDM corroborates D2–D3 conditions across Oahu through most of 2022, and federal agriculture disaster declarations were issued for every Hawaii county. Caveat: the reference panel skews toward modern automated networks (HydroNet, RAWS) and excludes legacy COOP sites. This may underrepresent low-elevation urban Honolulu, but the panel still spans the full windward-leeward and elevation gradient, which is what drives Oahu rainfall variability. The caveat is methodological honesty, not a substantive challenge to the result.
