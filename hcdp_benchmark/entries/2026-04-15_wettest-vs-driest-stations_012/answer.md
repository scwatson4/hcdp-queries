**Wettest station**: Piihonua (station 0213), Big Island, elevation 606m on the windward Hamakua coast. Estimated annual rainfall: 4,414mm/yr (174 in), with rain on 68.3% of days and a single-day max of 486mm.

**Driest station**: Kawaihae (station 0254), Big Island, elevation 113m on the leeward Kohala coast. Estimated annual rainfall: 353mm/yr (14 in), with rain on only 11.7% of days.

The wettest and driest stations on the entire network are on the same island, roughly 75km apart, yet Piihonua receives 12.5x more rainfall than Kawaihae.

### Key findings

- **Frequency, not intensity**: Wet stations get rain 68-71% of days; dry stations only 9-14%. The difference is how often it rains, not how hard.
- **Even dry stations can flood**: Lipoa (2nd driest, 494mm/yr) once received 203mm in a single day -- more than 40% of its entire annual total.
- **Geographic pattern**: All 5 wettest are windward sites; all 5 driest are leeward or rain-shadow locations.
- **Station 0115 excluded**: Piiholo (Maui) would appear as the wettest station by far due to 7999mm sensor error values that inflate its apparent rainfall to ~616,588mm/yr.
