The data is confirmed bad — both in our local copy and directly from the HCDP API. Station 0115 (Piiholo, Maui) reported exactly `7999` mm for 250 consecutive 5-minute intervals on March 5-6, 2023. This is a sensor error/saturation code (7999 mm in 5 minutes = 1.6 meters, physically impossible). The value 7999 is a classic max-value sentinel for a tipping-bucket rain gauge datalogger, meaning "value out of range" or "sensor fault." The station went offline entirely by March 8. HCDP has not flagged these readings (flag=0), so the bad data flows through to any downstream analysis unchecked.
